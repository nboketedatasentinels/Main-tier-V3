Status Report

The mid-flight report you'd send a steering committee on the project you scoped in Part B. Risk leads. Status follows. Decisions required are named, not buried.

Capstone Part C · Closes Project Leadership Discipline
What good looks like
A Status Report that protects the programme.
  • Risk leads — structured before being asked. Two named risks with severity, likelihood, mitigation.
  • Overall status confirmed clearly — with rationale, not just a colour.
  • Decisions required are named explicitly — specific asks of the committee.
  • Stakeholder positions distinguish healthy disagreement from political behaviour.
  • The Defensibility Statement is the test — would your position survive cross-examination without compromising?

Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Read Section 02 (Risk Register), Section 03 (Headline Status rationale), and Section 07 (Defensibility Statement) aloud, in that order. Don't summarise.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of the two risks you named, is there a third you didn't name because surfacing it would create a difficult conversation?
  • Your status rationale — is the colour you chose the one you actually believe, or the one that's easier to defend?
  • The Defensibility Statement — where in the actual committee meeting are you most likely to compromise?
