Take the opportunity you proposed in Part A and scope it end-to-end. Objectives, outcomes, timelines, budget, dependencies, methodology choice — with the rigour required to defend the plan to a steering committee.

Capstone Part B · Closes Lead Like a Pro
What good looks like
A Project Scope Document defensible to a steering committee.
  • Objectives and outcomes are distinguished — objectives are what the project does, outcomes are what changes for the organisation.
  • Timeline is realistic and includes the dependencies that could delay it — not a Gantt chart fantasy.
  • Budget is named with confidence level (your best estimate, your contingency, what you don't yet know).
  • Methodology choice (Waterfall / Agile / Hybrid) is justified by the work, not by personal preference.
  • The risk register names two real risks with severity, likelihood, and named mitigation owner — not "risks will be managed."

Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Read your methodology rationale, risk register, and steering defence aloud. Have your peer play the toughest steering committee member.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Your methodology choice — is the rationale specific to this work, or could it be cut-and-pasted onto any project?
  • The two risks in your register — if either materialised tomorrow, is your mitigation strong enough?
  • Your steering defence — when you imagine saying it aloud, do you soften under pressure, or hold the line?
