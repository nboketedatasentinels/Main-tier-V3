Opportunity Map

You'll identify three specific opportunities for AI or digital transformation in your current scope -- the kind of opportunities that exist in your organisation right now, that nobody has acted on yet, and that you can make a credible case for. The work is to train your eye for what most colleagues walk past.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • Specific opportunities -- named to a real system, process, team, or person. Not generic improvement ideas.
  • Cost of inaction estimated, even roughly. A real number beats abstract concern.
  • Honest read on why nobody has acted -- the political, capacity, or ownership dynamics, not just the technical reason.
  • An AI or digital transformation lens on each opportunity. Not a general improvement lens.
1
Step 1
Set Your Scope

Before mapping opportunities, give your assessor (and your peer in peer-to-peer) the context they need. Two short fields. Don't over-describe.

01
Your Role and Scope
The role you hold and the scope of work you're observing from. One or two sentences.
02
Industry or Sector
Helps the assessor calibrate the opportunities you'll identify. The same opportunity reads differently in mining vs banking vs healthcare.
2
Step 2
Map Opportunity One

The first opportunity in your scope. Probably the one that's been sitting there. Probably the one you've been talking about with colleagues for a while without doing anything about it. Three substeps -- what it is, what it costs to leave alone, why nobody has done it yet.

01
The Opportunity
In one sentence. No preamble. Name the thing.
02
Cost of Inaction
Estimate it. Hours per week, money per year, decisions delayed. Rough numbers beat abstract concern.
03
Why Nobody Has Done It
The honest answer. Politics, capacity, ownership, fear. This is what shows you understand the real organisation.
3
Step 3
Map Opportunity Two

Pick something different from Opportunity 1 -- a different team, a different process, or a different kind of value at stake. Same three substeps.

01
The Opportunity
In one sentence. Specific.
02
Cost of Inaction
In concrete terms.
03
Why Nobody Has Done It
The honest answer.
4
Step 4
Map Opportunity Three

Stretch a little. This one should be something you wouldn't normally raise because it's not "your" area. Notice what happens when you let yourself look there anyway.

01
The Opportunity
In one sentence. Stretch into territory that's not your remit.
02
Cost of Inaction
In concrete terms.
03
Why Nobody Has Done It
The honest answer.
5
Step 5
Choose the One You'd Stake Your Reputation On

Of the three opportunities you mapped, one stands out to you as the one you'd actually move on. Pick it. You'll carry this choice into Practical 2 (Stakeholder Position Paper) and the Capstone One-Page Proposal. Don't justify all three -- tell us why this one.

01
Which One
Select the opportunity you're taking forward.
02
Why This One
In two sentences. The rationale you write here is what you'll use to open conversations with stakeholders.
AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test Your Map

Practitioners are good at seeing what's broken. We're often less good at seeing what's broken that we've stopped noticing. Use AI as a thinking partner to surface opportunities your scope has gone blind to -- and to test the cost-of-inaction estimates you've made.

1
Surface What You've Stopped Seeing
Ask AI to challenge your scope by suggesting opportunities you haven't listed -- the ones that are too familiar to feel like opportunities anymore.
Paste this prompt
I'm a [your role] in a [your industry] organisation. The three AI/digital transformation opportunities I've identified in my scope are: [paste your three opportunities from Steps 2-4]. What three additional opportunities should I be considering that practitioners in roles like mine commonly miss? For each, explain why it's typically overlooked and where to find the evidence that it's worth paying attention to.
What did AI surface? Add to your map or note why you're not pursuing it.
2
Test Your Cost Estimates
Ask AI to evaluate your cost-of-inaction estimates for the opportunity you chose in Step 5. The goal is to find out if you're under-estimating or over-claiming before a stakeholder catches it.
Paste this prompt
Here is the opportunity I'm taking forward and the cost of inaction I've estimated: [paste your Step 5 choice and the Step 2-4 cost estimate for it]. Pressure-test this estimate. What assumptions am I making that might not hold? What costs am I likely missing? What costs am I likely overstating? Give me three specific questions a sceptical CFO would ask about this estimate.
What did AI flag? What will you adjust?
AI Lens
As a transformation practitioner, you'll increasingly use AI to surface, evaluate, and prioritise opportunities like the ones you've mapped here. What do you trust AI to do well in this kind of analysis -- and what specifically do you think you still need to do yourself, with the AI as input rather than output?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Hand these three questions to your peer.

Before you submit, talk this through with a peer from your cohort. Read your three opportunities aloud and ask them to put these questions to you. Most practitioners catch what's weak in their own work the moment a peer asks the right question -- which is the point.

  1. Of the three opportunities I named, which one feels least specific to you -- and what's the question I haven't answered yet?
  2. Look at the cost-of-inaction estimates. Which one would a sceptical CFO push back on, and what number am I missing?
  3. For the opportunity I'm taking forward in Step 5 -- if you were the decision-maker, what's the one thing about my read of the politics that doesn't ring true to you?
