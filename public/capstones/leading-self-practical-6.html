Accountability Brief

Six weeks of inner work doesn't sustain itself. The pattern, the protocol, the trigger map -- they all need someone outside your head to hold you to them when the room goes quiet and the old habits get loud. This Practical names that someone. The brief is real. The cadence is concrete. The first ask has been made -- or you commit to making it this week.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • A specific person named -- by role and relationship, not a hypothetical "trusted peer."
  • Concrete cadence -- "Tuesday text" or "monthly Thursday call," not "regular check-ins."
  • Truth-telling territory named -- the specific thing you'll tell them you won't tell colleagues.
  • A real ask -- one you've made, or one you commit to making this week.

1
Step 1
Name the Person

An accountability partner is not a mentor, not a coach, not a therapist. They are someone who can sit with the work you've done over six weeks and hold you to it without rescuing you from it. That's a specific kind of person. Name them.

01
Who They Are
Their role and your relationship -- not their name. One sentence.
02
Why Them Specifically
What makes them able to hold this work? Not what makes them nice -- what makes them able.
2
Step 2
Set the Cadence

"Regular check-ins" doesn't survive a busy quarter. Cadence has to be concrete enough that both of you know when the next one is without checking. Pick a rhythm you can actually keep -- if monthly is too much, weekly is too much.

01
The Rhythm
Concrete. "Tuesday text," "first Friday of the month, 30-min call," "quarterly half-day."
02
When You'll Skip It
What's the failure mode? Be honest about when this rhythm is most likely to break.
3
Step 3
Name the Truth-Telling Territory

Accountability without specific truth-telling territory becomes a friendship. Both of you need to know what specifically this person hears about that nobody else does. Vulnerability as strategy: not vulnerability everywhere, but vulnerability with one person about specific things.

01
What You'll Tell Them
The specific thing you'll tell them that you won't tell colleagues, family, or your manager.
02
What You Won't Ask Her To Do
Boundary check. What's not her job? Saves the relationship from drifting.
4
Step 4
Make the First Ask

Accountability that exists only in your head is not accountability. The first ask is what makes this real. Either it's already been made -- you've had the conversation -- or you commit to making it this week. No third option.

01
The Ask
In one sentence -- what specifically you've asked or will ask of them.
02
Status of the Ask
Already made (when, what they said) or commit to making it (by when, how).
5
Step 5
The Pattern Across the Six Weeks

Read your Practicals 1 through 5 again before answering this. Six weeks of inner work reveals patterns you couldn't see from inside any single Practical. Name one pattern you can see across the work -- specifically about how you lead under pressure when AI and digital transformation are reshaping the room.

01
The Pattern
Something that showed up across at least three of the five Practicals. Not generic. Reference the Practicals.
02
What This Means for AI / Digital Work
Specifically -- what does this pattern mean for how you'll lead in AI and digital transformation contexts going forward?
AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test the Brief

Accountability briefs are where senior practitioners write the version of accountability they wish they had -- generous, gentle, low-stakes. AI as an outside reader can hear when the brief is built to succeed or built to allow you to drift quietly.

1
Stress-Test the Cadence
Ask AI to evaluate whether your cadence will actually survive a difficult quarter -- or whether you've designed the version that breaks the moment things get hard.
Paste this prompt
I've designed an accountability rhythm for the next six months: [paste your Step 2 rhythm and failure mode]. Pressure-test this. Specifically: (1) When in the next six months is this rhythm most likely to break, and what's the specific scenario? (2) Is the failure-mode mitigation strong enough to survive the breakdown, or is it dependent on me being in a state where I don't need it most? (3) What's one structural change to the cadence that would make it harder to skip when things are hard?
What did AI flag? What will you adjust?
2
Test the Truth-Telling Territory
Ask AI to evaluate whether your truth-telling territory is real -- or whether you've described what you're already comfortable telling someone, which doesn't count.
Paste this prompt
My accountability brief names this as the truth-telling territory: [paste your Step 3]. Pressure-test it. Specifically: (1) Is this something I'm already comfortable telling someone, or is it actually new territory that takes courage? (2) What's one thing I'm carrying from the six-week Journey that I haven't named in this Practical -- the thing I'd tell my accountability partner only if I were being fully honest with myself? (3) Should that thing be added to the truth-telling territory, or kept private? Be honest. If I've written a comfortable version, say so.
What did AI surface? Will you adjust the truth-telling territory?

Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Accountability briefs are where the work either gets witnessed or quietly fades. A peer can hear when the brief names a real person and a real ask -- versus when it names a hypothetical relationship that's safer because nothing has been asked yet. This is the most important conversation of the six weeks.

What to bring

Read your Step 1 (who and why), Step 2 (cadence), Step 3 (truth-telling), and Step 4 (the ask) aloud, in order. Don't add context. Listen to your peer's reaction at Step 4. If you described an ask you haven't actually made yet, name that in the conversation -- and let them hold you to making it this week.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of the people you could have named, why this person? Are you sure they can sit with hard truths -- or did you pick them because they're easier on you?
  • Your truth-telling territory -- is it the real thing you've been carrying, or the comfortable version you've already half-said out loud?
  • If you said the ask hasn't been made yet -- what's the specific reason you haven't asked, and is that reason still valid by Friday?
