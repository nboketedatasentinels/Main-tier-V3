Digital Edge · AI Application

Build a one-page Mindset Action Plan from three weeks of work

Week 3 standardised Digital Edge for this Capstone
Why this matters: Most practitioners finish a course with a pile of notes and never convert them into something they use. This prompt does the conversion. You will not reread a journal -- you will reread a one-pager pinned to your desktop. The five minutes you spend with this prompt is the difference between practitioners who graduate and change, and practitioners who graduate and drift.
The prompt (copy-paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini)
You are a leadership coach. Based on what I share below, produce a one-page Mindset Action Plan with five sections: (1) The belief I'm changing, (2) The replacement belief, (3) Three triggers where the old belief used to run me, (4) The new behaviour I'm practicing, (5) One metric I'll track for 30 days. Keep it shorter than you want to. Material: [paste the patterns from your Pattern Profile, your protocol from your Protocol Card, and reflections from your peer sessions]
How to apply this to your Capstone: Run the prompt with your Practical 1, 2, and 3 content as input. Compare the AI's output with what you've written above in Sections 1-8. The four reflection questions below ask you to evidence your evaluative judgement of the AI output -- this is Outcome 6 evidence, and it's how the assessor knows you're using AI as a thinking partner, not a ghostwriter.
AI Integration · Outcome 6 evidence

Reflect on the Digital Edge Output

Outcome 6 evidences your evaluative judgement of AI -- not uncritical use. After running the Digital Edge prompt above, answer these four questions. Distinction-level work demonstrates judgement and shows what you applied AND what you critically rejected.

01 · What did the AI surface that you hadn't written down?
Something the prompt produced that you wouldn't have produced alone -- a structural reframe, a missed pattern, a tighter sentence.
02 · What did you apply from the AI output to this Plan?
Specific changes you made to your Capstone based on the AI version. Direct, concrete.
03 · What did you critically reject from the AI output?
Where the AI version didn't land. Distinction-level work shows evaluative judgement -- not deference, not wholesale acceptance.
04 · What does this teach you about how to use AI in your leadership work going forward?
One specific rule or discipline you're taking forward.

Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

The Mindset Action Plan is the half-time deliverable -- it closes Course 1 but not the Journey. A peer who's worked through Practicals 1-3 alongside you can hear when the Plan is genuinely a system versus a wishlist disguised as one. This conversation matters more than the previous Practical conversations because the stakes are higher.

What to bring

Read the patterns (Section 01) and the alternatives (Section 03) aloud, in pairs -- pattern then alternative. Don't summarise the calendar trigger or environmental change; let your peer ask if they want detail. The test is whether each pattern sounds like a real default of yours and each alternative sounds like something you'd actually run.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of your alternatives in Section 03, which one will you run first this week -- and which one are you secretly hoping you won't have to test soon?
  • Your calendar trigger and environmental change -- are they doing the work, or are they decorative? Could you remove one and the Plan would still hold?
  • If you told a sceptical version of yourself this Plan, where does it fall apart? That's the spot to tighten before submitting.
