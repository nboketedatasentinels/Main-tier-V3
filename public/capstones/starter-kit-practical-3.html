Methodology Justification

Choose the project methodology you'd run on the opportunity you've been working with -- Waterfall, Agile, or Hybrid -- and defend the choice. Not in textbook terms. In terms specific to your project, your team, and the AI or digital transformation context. The work is to own the choice well enough to defend it in a steering committee.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • Methodology choice defended with reasons specific to this project -- not generic methodology pros and cons.
  • Honest counter-argument considered. You can name the strongest case against your choice and how you'd respond.
  • AI / digital transformation risks specific to the methodology named -- model drift, adoption, data quality, regulatory exposure.
  • Defensible to a sceptical steering committee. Not "this is what we usually do."

The Strongest Counter-Argument

Someone will push back on your choice. If you can't articulate the strongest case against it, you don't yet understand the choice well enough to defend it. Steel-man the opposition. Then answer it.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test Your Methodology Choice

Methodology choice is one of those areas where senior practitioners get over-confident -- we've made the call so many times we forget to question it. AI as an outsider voice can surface what we've stopped seeing.

1
Stress-Test Your Choice Against Real Cases
Ask AI to find recent cases where your chosen methodology failed on a similar project -- and what those failures had in common.
Paste this prompt
I'm planning a [paste Step 1 project summary] using a [paste Step 2 methodology choice] approach. Find me three recent cases (last 5 years) where similar AI/digital transformation projects failed using this methodology. For each case, tell me: (1) what specifically went wrong, (2) whether the methodology choice contributed to the failure, and (3) what early warning signs the project leader missed. Be specific -- if you can't cite real cases, say so rather than make them up.
What did AI flag? What early warning signs will you watch for?
2
Test Your Counter-Argument
Ask AI to play the smartest sceptic on your steering committee and tell you whether your response to the counter-argument actually holds up.
Paste this prompt
I'm proposing a [methodology] approach. The strongest counter-argument I've identified is: [paste Step 4 counter-argument]. My response is: [paste Step 4 response]. Play a sceptical steering committee member who is not convinced. Press my response. What's the second-order pushback I haven't addressed? Where does my response sound rehearsed rather than thought-through?
Where did your response weaken? What will you tighten?
AI Lens
As AI tools get better at recommending project methodologies based on project characteristics, what's the difference between an AI's methodology recommendation and a transformation practitioner's choice? Where does practitioner judgement still earn its place?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Methodology choice is where senior practitioners most often signal certainty they don't have. A peer who knows your work but not your project can hear when you're defending the choice rather than understanding it. Bring this Practical to your peer-to-peer call this week.

What to bring

Read your Step 2 (the choice and headline reason), Step 3 (three reasons), and Step 4 (counter-argument and response) aloud. Don't pre-defend. Listen for where you sound less certain than you wrote -- that's where the real work is.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of your three reasons, which one is doing the most work? If we removed the other two, would the choice still hold?
  • Your counter-argument -- did you make it strong enough, or did you write a version of it you already knew how to answer?
  • If your COO asked you for the second-best methodology choice, what would you say? And how confident are you that you got the order right?
