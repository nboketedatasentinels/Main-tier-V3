Risk Register Draft

Build a working risk register for your project. Five risks. Each one with severity, likelihood, named owner, and a mitigation that can actually be executed. At least one risk must be specifically AI- or digital-transformation-flavoured -- not the generic project risks that show up on every register.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • Five risks specific to this project. Not "scope creep" or "stakeholder resistance" in generic form.
  • At least one AI-specific risk -- model drift, adoption, data quality, regulatory exposure, vendor lock-in, ethical exposure.
  • Every risk has a named owner -- a specific role. Not "the team" or "we." If everyone owns it, no one does.
  • Mitigations are actionable. Something the named owner could start on Monday, not a category of activity.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Find What Your Register is Missing

Risk registers tend to reflect the risks you've already seen -- in your industry, on your previous projects, from your team. AI can surface the categories you're blind to because they haven't bitten you yet.

1
Stress-Test Coverage
Ask AI to look at your five risks and tell you which standard risk categories you've under-covered or missed entirely.
Paste this prompt
I'm building a risk register for a [paste Step 1 project summary] using a [paste Step 1 methodology]. Here are the five risks I've identified: [paste your five risks from Step 2]. Look at standard risk categories for AI/digital transformation projects -- governance, data quality, model bias, regulatory, vendor lock-in, change management, security, ethical exposure. Tell me which categories I've covered, which I've under-weighted, and which I've missed. For any I've missed, suggest the specific risk I should add to my register and why.
What did AI flag? Will you update the register?
2
Pressure-Test Mitigations
Ask AI to evaluate whether your mitigations are actually actionable, or whether they're disguised wishful thinking.
Paste this prompt
Here are the five risks and mitigations from my register: [paste your five risks and mitigations from Step 2]. For each mitigation, tell me: (1) Is this actually actionable -- could the named owner start on it Monday? (2) Does it address the cause of the risk or just monitor for symptoms? (3) What's the specific failure mode this mitigation has -- where would it stop working? Be honest. If a mitigation is wishful thinking, say so.
Which mitigations did AI flag as weak? How will you tighten them?

Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Risk registers are where senior practitioners are most polite to themselves -- we list risks we've already partly mitigated and skip the ones that genuinely scare us. A peer can hear the difference. Bring this Practical to your peer-to-peer call this week.

What to bring

Read each risk aloud, including severity, likelihood, owner, and mitigation. Don't elaborate. Listen to your peer's questions. The most useful feedback usually starts with "and what about..." -- write down whatever follows.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of your five risks, which one keeps you up at night? Is it the one you marked High severity, or a different one you understated?
  • Every owner is named -- have you actually told them they own it, or are they about to find out from your register?
  • What risk should be on this register that isn't, because if you named it you'd have to do something uncomfortable about it?
