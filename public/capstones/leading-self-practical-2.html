Protocol Card

Build the small, named protocol you'll deploy when your pattern fires. One trigger. Three moves. A signal phrase. Then practice it for five days. This is the boring middle of any change -- repetition before results -- and it's where most leaders quit. Don't.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • Protocol is short -- a card you could keep on your desk. Three moves, not ten.
  • Tied to the pattern you named in Practical 1 -- not a generic "stay calm" card.
  • Includes a signal phrase -- something you say (out loud or to yourself) to interrupt the pattern in real time.
  • Practiced for at least three of five days, with honest notes on what landed and what didn't.

Five Days of Reps

A protocol you don't practice is just a piece of paper. For five days this week, deploy the protocol when the trigger fires. Capture what happened in 1-2 lines per day -- not a journal, just a log. If the trigger didn't fire on a given day, note that and write what you'd have done if it had. The repetition is the work. This is the boring middle that most people quit at.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Stress-Test the Protocol

A protocol that works on paper often falls apart under pressure. AI as a sparring partner can simulate the kind of moment where your protocol will be tested -- including the angles you didn't plan for.

1
Run the Protocol Through Three Hard Scenarios
Ask AI to throw three realistic scenarios at you that test your protocol -- the kind that would normally fire your pattern. Find out where the protocol is fragile before a real moment finds out for you.
Paste this prompt
I'm building a protocol to disrupt a leadership pattern. The pattern: [paste your Step 1 pattern]. The trigger: [paste your Step 1 trigger]. My protocol: [paste your Step 2 protocol -- name, three moves, signal phrase]. Generate three realistic high-pressure scenarios where this trigger would fire hard. For each, walk me through what I'd say and do using my protocol. Where does the protocol hold? Where does it likely break? What one adjustment would make it more durable?
What did AI flag? What will you adjust?
2
Test the Signal Phrase
A signal phrase that doesn't feel like yours won't fire when you need it. Ask AI to evaluate yours and offer two alternatives -- you might find the right one is the one you didn't write.
Paste this prompt
My signal phrase for activating a leadership protocol under pressure is: [paste your signal phrase]. For context, the protocol is about [paste protocol name and what it disrupts]. Evaluate this signal phrase. Does it sound like something I'd actually say to myself in a high-pressure moment, or does it sound like a slogan I'd find in a corporate training? Suggest two alternative signal phrases that might fit better -- and tell me what to listen for in choosing.
Did you keep your phrase or switch? Why?
AI Lens
As a transformation leader, you'll increasingly be expected to deploy yourself with discipline in moments where AI is also in the room -- a vendor pitch with AI tools demonstrating in real time, an exec review where AI dashboards are on screen, a board moment where someone uses AI to challenge your numbers. How does your protocol need to hold in those AI-saturated moments specifically?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

A protocol that exists only on paper is still theoretical. Your peer is the first person to hear you say it out loud as a deployable thing. The aliveness of your voice when you read it tells you whether the protocol is yours yet -- or still belongs to the page.

What to bring

Read the protocol card aloud -- name, three moves, signal phrase. Then read just one of your five rep entries. Don't explain. Listen to your peer's reaction. Their first observation usually catches whether the protocol is yours or imported.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Read your signal phrase aloud one more time. Does it sound like you, or like something a leadership book would say?
  • Of your five days, which day's note was hardest to write honestly? That's the day with the most to learn from.
  • If you ran this protocol for 30 more days, what's the version of you that would emerge? And do you actually want to be that version?
