The Adoption Curve Reading

One specific AI deployment in your organisation. The publicly cited adoption metric. What that metric is masking — pilot-stage performance, narrow user base, novelty effect, supervisor workaround, selective measurement. The genuine usage signal closer to the truth. The shift signals that would change your read. Then the recommended action.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • The cited metric is a specific publicly used figure — not a metric the practitioner thinks should be cited.
  • What the metric is masking is named with operational specificity — a pilot artefact, a workaround pattern, a selective measurement window.
  • The genuine usage signal is closer to the truth, not just more pessimistic. Substituting low numbers for high numbers is not analysis.
  • Shift signals work in both directions — specific things that would make you more confident in the cited number and specific things that would make you less confident.
  • An incoming CEO could verify or challenge the read using the shift signals.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test the Reading

Adoption Curve Readings fail in two predictable places — the practitioner substitutes pessimism for analysis, and the shift signals are written only in the direction that supports the read.

Test Whether the Genuine Signal Is Reasoning, Not Pessimism
Paste this prompt
The cited metric is [paste]. My claimed 'genuine usage signal' is [paste], because [paste]. Pressure-test: (1) Is the 'genuine signal' derived from a specific reframing of measurement (denominator change, definition tightening, opt-out adjustment) — or am I just substituting a smaller number for a larger one and calling that more honest? (2) Would an external auditor reading my methodology compute the number I am claiming? (3) Is my 'genuine signal' framed as a hostile correction or as a measurement frame that survives external audit? Tell me where the reasoning is pessimism in disguise.
Test Whether the Shift Signals Are Symmetrical
Paste this prompt
Up-signals: [paste]. Down-signals: [paste]. Pressure-test: (1) Are both lists equally specific in their named threshold? (2) Could an incoming CEO check both lists and reach the same conclusion the practitioner reaches? (3) Is one direction noticeably easier to reach than the other? Tell me where the signals are asymmetric and rewrite the weaker side.
AI Lens
Twelve months from now, when the AI-resolved-within-chat figure has either reached the cited metric (shifting you toward it) or stayed flat (confirming your read) — which of the three reasons you named for why the cited metric has held this long will turn out to have been the most consequential?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Adoption Curve Readings fall apart at the same place every time — the practitioner substitutes pessimism for analysis, and the shift signals are stronger in one direction than the other.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • The genuine usage signal — is it derived from a specific measurement reframing your peer can follow, or is it just a smaller number?
  • The shift signals in both directions — are they equally specific, or is one direction operational and the other rhetorical?
  • Your recommended action — if the dashboard change happens but the press cycle continues using the headline, has the action actually changed anything?
