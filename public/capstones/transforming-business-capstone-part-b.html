The Transformation Memo

A sponsor-facing operating memo written for one named executive sponsor. Five sections -- vision (with 24-hour recall data), resistance map (categorised), system reset moves, operating cadence (5 rhythms), executive ask -- plus the AI Integration section. The Memo must be sendable as is, with no further softening.
Outcomes assessed: LO3 (Construct and test a vision sentence), LO4 (Distinguish resistance categories), LO5 (Evaluate transformation system drift), LO6 (Integrate AI tools with judgement)
Word count: 800-1000 words total across all sections.
Capstone Part B of 2 · Combined Capstone = 50% of competence pass
What good looks like
A Memo sendable to a named executive sponsor -- vision tested, resistance categorised, reset moves named, cadence specific, ask explicit.
  • The vision sentence in Section 1 is the version that survived 24-hour recall, not the version that lands well in slides.
  • The resistance map in Section 2 distinguishes the four categories explicitly -- informed risk, fear, identity, political -- with category-matched moves for each.
  • The system reset moves in Section 3 address cause, not symptom -- structural changes derived from the Drift Heatmap.
  • The operating cadence in Section 4 scores all five rhythms with specifics, identifies the most-drifted one, and names the reset move with named participants and dates.
  • The executive ask in Section 5 has a specific date, a specific decision required, and a specific consequence of inaction.
  • The AI Integration in Section 6 demonstrates evaluative judgement -- what was kept, what was rejected, why -- not uncritical adoption.
  • The Memo as a whole could be sent to the named executive sponsor without further softening.

Outcome 3 · Vision

Vision -- with 24-hour recall data

State the transformation vision sentence (15-20 words maximum), then provide the 24-hour recall data from your three independent listeners in Practical 3. Include what each listener actually repeated back, what the data told you about the sentence's portability, and the revised version if revision was needed. The vision in this Memo is the version that survived recall.
Carry forwardCarry forward your vision sentence and 24-hour recall data from the Vision Sentence Test (Practical 3). The vision in the Memo is the recall-tested version, not the original draft.
The discipline is reporting the actual recall data, including failure to recall. A revised version that addresses the specific failure mode is stronger than an unrevised version that 'sort of' got through. The vision should be the one the team can carry, not the one that lands well in slides.

Section 02
Outcome 4 · Resistance map

Resistance map -- four categories with category-matched moves

Map the resistance you have encountered to this transformation across the four categories -- informed risk, fear, identity, political. For each category that is in play, name one specific resistance source (by role, situation, or pattern) and the category-matched move you are running for it. At least one resistance must be identified as informed risk and treated as a signal rather than a blocker.
The four categories produce structurally different resistance behaviours and require structurally different responses. Collapsing them into 'manage resistance' is the failure mode. For each category in play, the move named is specific to what that category requires -- not a general 'have a conversation'. Informed risk is the test of category discipline: it looks like resistance from the outside, but it is signal, and mishandling it is how transformations end up in regulatory or legal trouble.

Section 03
Outcome 5 · System reset moves

System reset moves -- cause, not symptom

Name two or three system reset moves derived from the Drift Heatmap (Practical 5). Each move addresses the cause of the drift -- not a symptom. The moves are structural (a process change, a decision-rights reallocation, an escalation pathway redesign), not motivational gestures or ambition escalations.
Carry forwardCarry forward findings from the Drift Heatmap (Practical 5). The reset moves should address the most-drifted rhythm you identified, not escalate the original ambition.
The test of cause-versus-symptom: would the move still operate if the team's energy dropped? Structural moves do; motivational ones do not. Ambition escalation (more meetings, harder targets) is the most common failure mode and should be named explicitly if you are tempted toward it.

Section 04
Outcome 5 · Operating cadence

Operating cadence -- the five rhythms

Score the transformation operating system across the five rhythms -- decision cadence, communication cadence, escalation discipline, learning cadence, reset trigger -- as green/amber/red against observable evidence in the past 60 days. Identify the most-drifted rhythm and the specific reset action booked, including by when, with whom, and what success looks like in 30 days.
All five rhythms scored against observable evidence, not gut feel. The most-drifted rhythm is named honestly, including if it is uncomfortable to name. The reset action is in your calendar, with named participants -- not 'I will set up a meeting'. Success in 30 days is observable to someone other than you.

Section 05
Outcome 5 · Executive ask

Executive ask -- specific, time-bound, with a consequence

State the executive ask. The ask names the named executive sponsor, a specific decision required by a specific date, and the specific consequence of inaction. The Memo lives or dies on this section -- if the ask is generic, the Memo is information rather than a memo.
Three elements -- decision, date, consequence -- each named with operational specificity. The decision is one the executive can make in a single yes-or-no conversation, not a portfolio of moves. The date is real. The consequence is concrete -- not 'risk to the transformation' but 'we miss the regulatory window in Q3' or 'the Nigeria GM rolls back to legacy by 30 June'. Without a consequence, the executive has no reason to act on the date.

Section 06
Outcome 6 · AI Integration

AI Integration -- judgement, not adoption

Select one Digital Edge exercise output from this Module (the resistance categorisation prompt, the vision stress-test prompt, the Drift Heatmap prompt, or another). Name what you kept from the AI output, what you rejected, and why -- including any instance where you overrode the AI suggestion with your own judgement.
The discipline is evaluative -- not 'AI gave me good ideas'. Name a specific output, what you kept and why, what you rejected and why. If everything was kept, you have summarised, not evaluated. The override case is the strongest evidence: where the AI suggested one move and you chose another, with the reasoning named.
