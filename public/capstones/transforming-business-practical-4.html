The Resistance Reframe

Pick the resister you have been treating as a blocker -- the one who has been pushing back on the AI rollout, slow-walking the regulator response, raising the same question for the third time, or going quiet at the moments you most needed them in. Run the conversation with them. Then come back and reflect on what category the resistance was actually in -- informed risk, fear, identity, or political -- and what shifted when you made the category-matched move instead of your default response. Reflection is the Practical -- the conversation is the cost of admission.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • The resister is named -- by initial or short reference -- and the AI-era dynamic the resistance attaches to is specific (rollout pushback, regulatory concern, vendor scope creep, board scepticism, middle-management slow-walk).
  • The category you had been making is named honestly -- including if the default category was 'this person is a blocker' rather than a real diagnostic read.
  • The category the resistance was actually in is one of the four -- informed risk, fear, identity, political -- not a hybrid or 'all of the above'.
  • The category-matched move is named with operational specificity -- not 'I listened more', not 'I was empathetic'. A specific change in how you ran the conversation.
  • What is now in motion is a concrete next step -- a meeting booked, a memo redrafted, an escalation withdrawn, an authority recognised in writing -- with a date.
1
Step 1
Set Up the Resistance Before You Reflect

Before the five-stage reflection, two pieces of context. Who the resister is -- by initial or short reference -- and the AI-era dynamic the resistance attaches to. The category-matched move is only credible if a peer can see what was at stake, and what would have been at stake if the resistance had been read wrong for another month.

01
The Resister
By initial or short reference. Their role. Their formal authority on the rollout. Their relationship to you. Whether you initiated the conversation or they did.
02
The AI-Era Dynamic
What about this resistance is AI-era specific? Not a generic management challenge -- the dynamic that exists because the rollout is AI.
2
Step 2
Reflect Across the Five Stages

Walk the resistance reframe in five stages. The order is the diagnostic logic. Stages 2 and 3 are where the Practical lives -- the read you had been making, against the read you should have been making. Resist the temptation to write Stage 2 (your default read) in retrospect-friendly language. The point of the Practical is that you had been reading it wrong, until the conversation forced the recategorisation.

The Resistance Reframe Anatomy
Five stages · in order · the recategorisation moment in Stage 3 is the work
01
Who and What's at Stake
The resister, named. The specific decision or programme momentum that their resistance has been affecting. What would happen in the next 30 days if the resistance had not been addressed.
02
The Read You Had Been Making
The category your default frame had put the resistance into. Honest. Most defaults are 'this person is a blocker' or 'this is fear of the new' -- neither is a real diagnostic read.
03
The Read You Should Have Been Making
The actual category -- one of informed risk, fear, identity, political. Pick the category below to lock the read. What was the resistance carrying that you had been missing?
Lock the Category
Which of the four categories was the resistance actually in?
Pick one. The category-matched move in Stage 4 has to align to it. Hybrid categories are usually a sign the read is not yet honest.
04
The Category-Matched Move
What you did in the conversation that you would not have done if you had stayed with your default read. Specific. Operational. The move that matches the category, not a general 'I listened better'.
05
What Is Now in Motion
One specific thing. A meeting booked, a memo redrafted, an authority recognised in writing, an escalation withdrawn. With a date. Not 'we agreed to keep talking'.
3
Step 3
Name the Cost of the Original Misread

The category-matched move is the work. The cost of having had the wrong category for the previous nine weeks (or however long) is the lesson. Two checks. First: what would have happened in the next 30 days if you had continued with the misread? Second: where else in your week is the same default category likely to be misreading another resistance? Naming the second is what makes the Practical generalise. Without it, you have reframed one resistance, not changed an instinct.

01
Cost of Continuing the Misread
If you had continued treating this resistance as your default category for another 30 days -- what specifically would have failed?
02
Where Else the Same Default May Be Misreading
Where else in your week is the same default category likely to be miscategorising another resistance? One specific name or role.
AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test the Recategorisation

Resistance Reframes fail in two predictable places -- the practitioner picks the safest category in retrospect (often 'informed risk', because it flatters the resister), and the category-matched move is described in language soft enough that it would match any of the four categories. AI as an outside reader catches both. Use it before you submit.

1
Test Whether the Category Is the Honest One
Ask AI to test whether the category you settled on is the genuinely correct one -- or the most flattering one to the resister and the practitioner.
Paste this prompt
I am reframing a resistance I had been misreading. The resister is: [paste]. The default read I had been making was: [paste]. The read I now believe is correct is [category]. The evidence I am using: [paste]. Pressure-test the recategorisation on three criteria. (1) Is the new category the one a third-party assessor would arrive at independently, or is it the one most flattering to the resister and to me? (2) Could the same evidence support a different category -- and if so, which one, and what would have to be true about the resister's behaviour to confirm the alternative? (3) The evidence I cite -- is it specific to this case, or could it be applied to any of the four categories with minor rewording? Tell me where the read might still be defensive and what would make it more rigorous.
Where might the recategorisation still be defensive? What is the more honest read?
2
Test the Category-Matched Move for Specificity
Ask AI to test whether the move you describe is genuinely category-matched -- or whether it would match any of the four categories with the names changed.
Paste this prompt
My category-matched move was: [paste]. The resistance was in category [category]. Pressure-test on three criteria. (1) If the resistance had actually been fear, would my move have been the same -- or substantially different? (2) If it had been identity, would my move have been the same -- or substantially different? (3) If my described move could fit three of the four categories, it is not yet category-matched -- it is just 'I listened more thoughtfully'. Identify which words in the move description make it specifically informed-risk-matched, and which words would survive any category. Rewrite the move with the category-specific words tightened.
Where is the move not yet category-matched? What is the rewritten version?
AI Lens
Six months from now, when the next regulator window opens or the next AI-era pressure cycle compounds, which of the four resistance categories are you most likely to default-miscategorise? And what is the trigger that should override the default next time?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Resistance Reframes fall apart at the same place every time -- the practitioner picks the most flattering category in retrospect, and the category-matched move is described in language soft enough that it would fit any category. A peer who has reframed their own resistance can hear both at the same speed an assessor will. Bring this to your peer-to-peer call this week.

What to bring

Read out the default read first (Stage 2) before the corrected read. If your peer's first reaction is 'that read makes the resister sound like a problem' -- the default was operating in the role you needed to recategorise out of. Then read the category-matched move (Stage 4). Ask your peer whether the move would have looked different under any of the other three categories. If they say 'I think this would match fear or identity too' -- the move is not yet category-specific.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • The category you settled on -- is it the one the evidence supports, or the one most flattering to the resister? Could the same evidence support a different category?
  • Your category-matched move -- if you read it back without the category label, would your peer be able to identify which of the four it was matched to? If not, the move is generic.
  • The other place you flagged the same default may be misreading -- if you do not have a conversation with that person in the next two weeks, what is the cost of carrying the misread for another month?
