The case · read first
Standard Bank Group, 2020-2025 -- and the cascade across 21 markets
Collapse
Background

Standard Bank Group is Africa's largest bank by assets, headquartered in Johannesburg, South Africa, with operations in 21 sub-Saharan African countries. CEO Sim Tshabalala, who became Group CEO in 2013, has led an extensive digital and AI-led transformation programme through the SBG 2025 strategy and into the bank's 2028 targets.

Public reporting documents the strategic direction with notable consistency. Tshabalala has stated explicitly across multiple interviews -- with HBR IdeaCast, Daily Investor, and InfotechLead in 2024 and 2025 -- that the bank's stance is to stay close to AI developments without being on the cutting edge. His phrase, repeated in multiple settings: 'we do not want to pay first mover taxes.' He describes AI as 'a tool to meet human needs' rather than as a force that will 'obliterate banking, insurance, and asset management.'

By the bank's FY25 reporting, 71% of migratable servers had been moved to cloud and 88% of legacy infrastructure had been decommissioned. AI deployment has focused on credit decisioning, fraud detection, and personalised financial services -- with CIO Jörg Fischer overseeing the technology transformation. Standard Bank serves more than 20 million active clients; about 99% of customer interactions occur through digital channels.

The transformation has been judged externally as successful. Tshabalala was named Africa's CEO of the Year in 2025 by The Asian Banker for driving pan-African integration, digital transformation, and sustainable growth. He has announced his retirement at end of 2027, with a CEO and CFO transition scheduled.

Inside the bank's 21 markets, however, public commentary from former and current employees on professional networks documents a pattern that has emerged across the rollout. The original transformation vision -- articulated by Tshabalala in 2020-2021 around becoming 'a client-centred platform business' that delivers 'individualised, instantly available solutions' -- was clear, repeatable, and widely shared at launch. By 2024, in country teams in Nigeria, Kenya, and Uganda, the version of the vision being repeated by middle management had drifted significantly: the 'client-centred platform' framing had been replaced in many internal communications by efficiency, productivity, and cost-reduction language tracked to specific operational metrics. Senior practitioners reported in industry forums (including the 2024 African Banking Conference in Cape Town) that the vision team members could repeat 24 hours after a town hall was no longer the original Tshabalala vision -- it was the operational metric closest to their current bonus.

The dynamic to analyse

This case is not a story about whether Standard Bank's AI strategy is right -- by external measures it is performing well. It is a story about what happens to a transformation vision as it cascades through 21 markets and multiple management layers, and about the difference between the vision an executive articulates in interviews and the vision team members can repeat 24 hours after hearing it.

The questions below ask you to analyse the room Tshabalala has been walking into across multiple country markets, identify why the vision drifted in specific layers, and design a Connection Blueprint that would have travelled better across the layers.

Question 01
Outcome 1 · Door Practice

Read the room Tshabalala is walking into in a country market

When Tshabalala walks into a country market town hall in Nigeria or Kenya in 2024, what is the room he is actually walking into -- in terms of AI fluency, the listener's actual top priority that quarter, and the vendor narrative that has shaped middle-management framing of the AI rollout? Use the Door Practice framework.
Three things to read -- AI fluency, top priority, vendor narrative. Each one named with specificity to a real country (the case anchors three: Nigeria, Kenya, Uganda). "Top priority" is the priority for the listener that quarter, not the priority for head office. The asymmetric AI fluency is the test -- the practitioner has to be honest about whether the room has more or less fluency than they themselves do.

Question 02
Outcome 1 · Drift layer

Why did the 'client-centred platform' vision drift to operational metric language?

Why did the original 'client-centred platform' vision drift to operational metric language in middle management communications? Identify the specific layer where the drift happened and the dynamic that caused it.
A specific transition between management layers -- not "the message got lost over time". The dynamic that caused it is structural (incentive structure, communication asymmetry, KPI translation, or bonus alignment), not "managers did not communicate well". The test is whether your answer points to where intervention has to happen, not who to blame.

Question 03
Outcome 2 · Connection Blueprint

Construct the four-section Blueprint addressed to a country GM

Construct a four-section Connection Blueprint -- Position, Now, Stake, Survival -- that Tshabalala (or his Group Strategy team) could have used in 2022 to communicate the vision to country market General Managers in language matched to their actual incentive structure. Name a specific country GM by role, their priority that quarter, and the language match.
Four sections, each addressed to a named country GM by role. Position anchors to the programme's actual state, not the press-release version. Now anchors to a specific business cycle moment for that GM (a regulatory window, a competitor move, a board cycle), not generic urgency. Stake names what is at stake for the GM specifically. Survival names what survival of the transformation requires from the GM in language they would use about their own work.

Question 04
Outcomes 1 + 2 · Application to your own scope

Apply the same discipline to a vision drift in your own scope

Identify a transformation vision in your own current organisational scope where the version being repeated 24 hours after the town hall is not the version you articulated. Name the layer where the drift is happening and the one Connection Blueprint section you will rewrite this week to address it.
This is the highest-weighted question. The vision drift needs to be real (something you have actually heard repeated back differently from how you said it), the layer needs to be specific (which transition between layers), and the Blueprint section you rewrite is one of the four (Position, Now, Stake, Survival) -- not all four. The rewrite happens this week, in your calendar.
