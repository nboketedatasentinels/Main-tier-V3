Lessons Synthesis

Look back across the five Practicals you've built over this Journey. Find the patterns -- in your work, in your blind spots, in how you handle pressure. Name the gap that's still real. Commit to one growth edge for your AI / digital transformation work over the next 90 days. This is the Practical that connects everything.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • References the previous five Practicals by name and content -- not generic reflection on "the Journey."
  • Names a real pattern across the work, not a single highlight from one Practical.
  • Identifies a specific gap -- something you actually didn't do well, not a polished "growth area."
  • Commits to one concrete growth edge for AI / digital transformation work, with a 90-day horizon.

Step 1
Find the Patterns Across the Five

Read your Practicals 1-5 again before answering. Look for what shows up more than once -- a kind of thinking, a kind of avoidance, a kind of strength. Name two patterns. Don't summarise the work -- find what runs through it.

Pattern 1
Something that showed up across at least three of the five Practicals. Could be a strength (you consistently identified the political layer), or a tendency (you consistently softened your asks). Reference the Practicals by name.
Pattern 2
A second pattern -- ideally a different kind from Pattern 1. If Pattern 1 was a strength, see if you can find a tendency. If Pattern 1 was a tendency, see if you can find a strength. Honesty matters more than balance.

Step 2
The Practical Where You Worked Hardest

Step 3
Name the Gap That's Still Real

Six weeks in, what gap haven't you closed? The one that would still show up if your COO put you in front of an actual transformation problem next week. Be specific -- "I'm still learning AI" is the wrong answer. The right answer names a specific capability with a specific consequence.

The Gap
One paragraph. Name the capability gap, give one example of where it would bite, and resist the urge to soften it with "but I'm working on..."

One specific commitment for the next 90 days that closes some of the gap from Step 3. Specific enough that someone reading it could ask you in 90 days "did you do it?" and you could answer yes or no. Tied to your AI / digital transformation work, not generic professional development.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test the Synthesis

Synthesis is where practitioners most often write the version of themselves they wish they were rather than the version they are. AI as an outsider voice can hear the difference -- if you're willing to feed it the actual material.

1
Test Your Patterns Against the Material
Ask AI to read your Practicals 1-5 and tell you whether the patterns you named in Step 1 are actually visible in the work -- or whether you wrote what you wished was true.
Paste this prompt
I've completed five Practicals over a six-week Power Journey on AI / digital transformation practice. The two patterns I've named across them are: [paste your Step 1 patterns]. Here are the five Practicals: [paste a 2-3 line summary of each Practical's content]. For each pattern I named, tell me: (1) is the pattern actually visible in the material, (2) is there a stronger pattern I missed, and (3) did I name a real pattern or a flattering one? Be honest. If I wrote what I wished was true, say so.
What did AI flag? Will you adjust your patterns?
2
Stress-Test the Commitment
Ask AI to evaluate whether your 90-day commitment is actually doable -- or whether you're setting up to quietly fail.
Paste this prompt
My 90-day commitment from this Journey is: [paste your Step 4 commitment]. My first action this week is: [paste your Step 4 first action]. Pressure-test the commitment. Specifically: (1) Is the scope realistic for a senior practitioner with an existing day job? (2) What's the most likely reason I won't actually do this? (3) What's one thing I should add or change to make it more likely I'll keep the commitment? Be specific.
What did AI flag? What will you adjust?

Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Synthesis is where senior practitioners are most polite to themselves -- we round off the rough edges and tell ourselves a flattering story about the work. A peer who has done their own Journey can hear the gap between what's on the page and what's actually true. This is the most important conversation of the six weeks.

What to bring

Read your two patterns from Step 1, your Step 3 gap, and your Step 4 commitment aloud. Pause after each. Don't add context. Listen to your peer's reaction. The thing you most want to defend is usually the thing that needs more honesty.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of the two patterns you named, which one was easier to write? The easy one is usually the flattering one -- is it actually the truer one?
  • Your gap -- is it the real one, or the one that's safer to admit because it's already on your development plan?
  • Your 90-day commitment -- if I asked you in 30 days whether you've done it, what's the most likely answer? If "I started but..." is on the table, what's missing from the commitment?
