The Data Foundation Audit

For one stated AI use case. Five data dimensions: sources, quality, freshness, governance permissions, lineage. For each dimension: what the use case actually needs versus what the organisation actually has — with concrete evidence. The gap. The cost of closing it. At least one gap the organisation is currently downplaying. Then the recommended call to leadership: deploy now, deploy after foundation work, or do not deploy and reframe.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • The use case is the same one mapped in Week 3 — so the audit reads as the next layer down, not a fresh exercise.
  • For each dimension, 'data needed' describes what the use case actually requires — not what the organisation wishes was the requirement.
  • 'Data have' cells contain concrete artefacts: a named system, a named data steward, a real lineage diagram, a documented audit.
  • At least one gap is flagged as currently downplayed — including the political reason it has been kept off the public agenda.
  • Cost of closing each gap is named in time, money, and dependency terms — so a CFO can size the trade-off without coming back for clarification.
  • The recommended call (deploy / after foundation / reframe) is defensible to a CFO who is going to ask 'why this call and not the other two'.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Pressure-Test the Audit and the Call

Data Foundation Audits fail in two predictable places — the 'data have' column is scoped to data the practitioner has line-of-sight to (so structural gaps are mistaken for inventory gaps), and the recommended call is the one most palatable to the current sponsor rather than the one the audit actually supports.

Test Whether the Audit Inventoried the Real Data Estate
Paste this prompt
I have audited five data dimensions for an AI use case. The 'data have' cells are: [paste]. Pressure-test: (1) For each row, am I describing what exists across the organisation — including data held by adjacent functions, legacy systems, third-party processors, and shadow datasets — or what I personally have line-of-sight to? (2) For each row, is there a system, processor, or function I should have asked but did not? Name it. (3) The data I described as 'not present' — is any of it actually present somewhere I have not looked? Tell me which row's inventory is most likely to be incomplete.
Test Whether the Call Is the One the Audit Supports
Paste this prompt
My audit identifies these structural gaps: [paste]. My recommended call is [deploy now / deploy after / reframe]. Rationale: [paste]. Pressure-test: (1) Imagine the audit was identical but the practitioner had no political relationship with the current sponsor. Would the call still be the same? (2) Imagine the audit identified one fewer structural gap. Would the call still be the same? (3) The call I did not pick that would have been more aggressive — what is the one piece of audit evidence that, if 30% worse than written, would force me to that other call? Tell me where the call is sponsor-aware rather than audit-driven.
AI Lens
Twelve months from now, when the foundation workstream has run for nine months and the regulator response has come back — which dimension of this audit will have aged worst, and which gap that you flagged today will be the one the regulator will have asked about that nobody on the executive committee was prepared for?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Data Foundation Audits fall apart at the same place every time — the 'data have' column ends up describing the practitioner's line-of-sight rather than the organisation's actual inventory, and the recommended call is sponsor-aware rather than audit-driven. This is the heaviest week of the Journey — bring it to your peer-to-peer call with extra time scheduled.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of your five 'data have' cells, which one is most likely to be your line-of-sight rather than the organisation's actual data estate? Who should you have asked, and have you?
  • The downplayed gap you flagged — can you name the specific person or function who would be uncomfortable if it became a board agenda item this quarter?
  • If one piece of audit evidence got 30% worse, which call would you escalate to? If you cannot answer that, the call is not falsifiable.
