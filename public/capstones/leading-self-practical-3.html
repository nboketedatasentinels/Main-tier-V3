Mindset Action Plan Draft

A working draft of the plan you'll deliver as your Capstone. Not the final version. The version that has your honest first thinking on it -- the belief you're changing, the replacement, the behaviour you'll practice, the metric you'll hold yourself to. The Capstone polishes this. The draft surfaces what you actually believe.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • Clear progression from Practical 1's belief to a named replacement -- not a generic "growth" statement.
  • The new behaviour you'll practice is specific. Drawn from your Practical 2 protocol, not invented fresh.
  • One metric you can actually track over 30 days. Observable. Honest. Not "feel more confident."
  • A draft, not a polished page. Honesty matters more than presentation -- the Capstone is where you polish.

Step 2
Name the Replacement Belief

A belief you're changing has to be replaced with something -- not just removed. The replacement isn't an affirmation. It's a working belief you can act from. Specific to your true belief, written in language you'd actually say to yourself, and credible enough to hold in a real moment.

The Replacement Belief
In your own voice. Not "I am enough." Something specific to the true belief you named. The test: would you say this aloud to a peer without flinching at the language?
Why This Replacement and Not a Generic One
Two sentences. Why this specific replacement is the right one for the specific true belief you named -- not a one-size-fits-all confidence affirmation.
3
Step 3
The New Behaviour You're Practicing

Belief change without behaviour change is just a thought you had. The new behaviour you'll practice for the next 30 days has to be specific, observable, and drawn from the protocol you tested in Week 2. This is what the rest of the Journey -- and the Capstone -- will hold you to.

The Behaviour
Specific. Observable. Tied to your protocol. The version a peer could spot in a meeting and say "yes, she's doing the new thing."
Three Triggers to Watch For
The specific situations in the next 30 days where this behaviour will be tested. From your real calendar, not hypothetical scenarios.
4
Step 4
One Metric You'll Track for 30 Days

A plan without a metric is a wish. One metric you can track. Observable. Honest. Not feelings-based -- "I feel more confident" doesn't count, because we're terrible at remembering how we felt three weeks ago. Something you can put a tally mark next to. The metric is what makes this real on Day 30.

The Metric
Specific. Observable. Counted, not assessed. Something you could share with a peer at Day 30 and they could verify.
What 'Success' Looks Like at Day 30
If you achieve the metric, what observable difference will be visible -- to you, to your team, to your stakeholders? One sentence each.
AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Tighten the Draft

A first draft is the version of yourself you're hoping is true. AI as an outside reader can help you find where the draft is still hedging -- and where it could be sharper without losing your voice.

1
Stress-Test the Replacement Belief
Ask AI to read your true belief and your replacement together, and tell you whether the replacement actually answers the true belief -- or just sounds nice next to it.
Paste this prompt
I'm writing a Mindset Action Plan as a transformation leader. The true belief I'm changing is: [paste your Step 1 belief]. The replacement belief I'm working with is: [paste your Step 2 replacement]. Pressure-test this. (1) Does the replacement actually answer the true belief, or are they on different topics? (2) Is the replacement specific enough to act from in a real moment, or is it still a slogan? (3) What's the most honest version of this replacement I could write -- the one I haven't quite written yet?
What did AI flag? Will you adjust your replacement?
2
Test the Metric
Ask AI to evaluate whether your metric will actually work -- or whether you've set up something that'll quietly fall apart by Day 14.
Paste this prompt
My 30-day metric for tracking a leadership behaviour change is: [paste your Step 4 metric]. (1) Will I actually track this for 30 days, or is the tracking system itself too cumbersome? (2) Is the metric specific enough that I can answer 'yes' or 'no' on each occurrence -- or is there ambiguity that will let me grade myself generously? (3) What's the failure mode -- the most likely reason this metric stops getting tracked by Day 14?
What did AI flag? Will you adjust the metric?
AI Lens
As you start using AI more in your transformation work, AI will be in many of the moments where this new behaviour will be tested. How does the Mindset Action Plan you're drafting need to hold up specifically when AI is in the room with you -- producing data, generating responses, accelerating the pace of senior conversation?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

A draft read aloud is different from a draft on the page. Your peer hears the conviction (or absence of it) in your voice when you say the replacement belief. They notice when the metric is something you'd actually track versus something you wrote because you needed a metric. This conversation is what tells you whether the draft is ready for the Capstone.

What to bring

Read your replacement belief aloud, then your new behaviour, then your metric. Don't add commentary. Listen for where your voice loses energy -- that's the part of the draft that's still wishful, and the part the Capstone needs to either tighten or rewrite.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Read your replacement belief aloud once more. Did you sound like you believed it, or like you were trying it on?
  • Your metric -- if I asked you on Day 14 whether you've been tracking it, what's the honest answer? If "I've been meaning to" is on the table, what's wrong with the metric?
  • If your true belief surfaced again in a senior meeting tomorrow, would the protocol you've drafted hold? Or is there one move missing that you already know about?
