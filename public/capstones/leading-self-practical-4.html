Carrying Inventory

Take stock of what you're carrying into high-stakes meetings -- the masks, the inherited expectations, the unspoken assumptions about what a leader 'should' look like. Then choose what to put down. Authority and Presence is not about adding more. It's about leading from less, more anchored.

What good looks like
Aim for these as you go.
  • Three real masks named -- not abstract concepts. The specific behaviours you put on for high-stakes rooms.
  • Each mask traced to where it came from -- the moment, the role, the message you absorbed.
  • Three things to put down, with specific reason -- because it's costing more than it's protecting.
  • One anchor -- something true about you that you'll lead from instead of leading from the mask.

Step 3
Name Your Anchor

If you're putting down the masks, you need something to lead from instead. The anchor is something true about you, your work, or your value that you stand on when the room gets pressured. Not an affirmation. A fact you can hold. The cleaner the anchor, the more deliberate your presence becomes.

AI-Powered Activity

Use AI to Test the Inventory

Inventories are easier to write than to enact. The mask we list as "putting down" tends to be the easy one -- the ones we're not yet ready to admit stay invisible. AI as an outside reader can hear what's missing.

1
Test What You've Left Out
Ask AI to read your three masks and three things you're putting down, and tell you what category of mask you might still be carrying that you haven't named.
Paste this prompt
I'm a transformation leader doing inventory work on what I bring into high-stakes meetings. The three masks I've named that I'm carrying are: [paste your three "Carrying" entries from Step 2]. The meeting context: [paste Step 1 meeting]. Read these carefully. (1) What category of mask might I be carrying that I haven't named? (2) Of the three I've listed, which one do you suspect I named because it was easier to admit -- and what's the harder one underneath? (3) What's the question I should be asking myself that I haven't yet?
What did AI surface? Will you add to your inventory?
2
Pressure-Test the Anchor
Ask AI whether your anchor will actually hold under the specific pressure of your meeting -- or whether it's an anchor you'd need to embellish to use.
Paste this prompt
My anchor for an upcoming high-stakes meeting is: [paste your Step 3 anchor]. The meeting context: [paste Step 1 meeting]. Pressure-test the anchor. (1) Is this anchor specific and verifiable, or is it general enough that I'd need to invent details to use it? (2) Will it hold when the CFO's scepticism actually fires -- or is it the kind of anchor that sounds strong on paper but evaporates under questioning? (3) What's a sharper version of this anchor I could use?
What did AI flag? Will you sharpen the anchor?
AI Lens
In an AI-augmented organisation, leaders are increasingly being told to "be more like the AI" -- decisive, fast, data-grounded. What's the risk of trying to lead from an AI's posture instead of from your own anchor -- and what does staying anchored look like when AI is constantly modelling a different kind of presence in the room?
Take This to Peer-to-Peer

Before you submit, talk it through.

Inventory work is where senior practitioners hide the heaviest items behind the easiest ones. A peer who's done their own inventory can hear which mask you named first because it was safe -- and which one you didn't name because it's still too close to admit. The conversation is the test of whether your inventory is honest.

What to bring

Read your three "Carrying" entries aloud, then your anchor. Don't read the "Putting Down" column -- let your peer guess what you'd put down based on what you said. The gap between their guess and your actual list tells you which masks you're protecting.

Three questions for your peer to ask you
  • Of your three masks, which one is easiest to put down? That's probably not the one to focus on -- the easy one was already on its way out.
  • What mask did you almost write but didn't? Why not?
  • Your anchor -- when the meeting actually pressures it, what's the most likely reason it'll wobble? And what do you need to add to it now to prevent that?
