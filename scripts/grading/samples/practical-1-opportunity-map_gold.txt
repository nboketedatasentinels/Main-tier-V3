opportunity_3: Preventive-maintenance scheduling is reactive: 22% of services happen after a breakdown rather than on plan, pulling vehicles off the road unpredictably. Cost of inaction: roughly $450k/yr in emergency repairs and missed deliveries, plus unmeasured reliability damage with two anchor contract customers.

chosen_opportunity: Opportunity 1: closing the fleet-utilisation gap by replacing the ignored 14-export weekly report with an automated weekly action dashboard.

why_chosen: It carries the largest cost of inaction ($1.2M/yr vs $700k and $450k), it sits squarely in the COO's Operating Leverage cost programme so it has a sponsor already looking for exactly this, and it is the most within my direct scope as Operations Analytics Lead: I own the data and the report, so I can move without waiting on depot managers or maintenance vendors. The ROI is also the most defensible because the baseline (67% for 11 months) is already measured and uncontested.
