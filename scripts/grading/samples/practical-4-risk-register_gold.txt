project_summary: A 90-day, $35k plus 0.4 FTE pilot that consolidates fleet, driver and customer data into an automated weekly action dashboard on our existing platforms, targeting a fleet utilisation lift from 67% to 70% (about $1.2M/yr).

methodology: Hybrid: Waterfall governs the data-integration layer (fixed scope across the three source systems, sequenced and signed off once), while Agile governs the reporting layer (the weekly action dashboard ships in two-week increments against COO feedback).

risk_1: Risk: The three source systems (telematics, driver roster, customer ERP) hold inconsistent vehicle and route identifiers, so the consolidated utilisation figure is wrong and the COO loses trust in the dashboard. | Type: Standard | Severity: High | Likelihood: High | Owner: Operations Analytics Lead | Mitigation: Run a two-week reconciliation sprint in days 1 to 14, agree a single master vehicle ID with the Fleet Systems Administrator, and gate go-live on a 98% match rate against a manual sample of 30 vehicles.

risk_2: Risk: The 0.4 FTE is shared with live operations, so peak-season demand pulls the analyst off the pilot and the build stalls past day 90. | Type: Standard | Severity: High | Likelihood: Medium | Owner: COO | Mitigation: Ring-fence the 0.4 FTE in writing with the line manager, agree a named backup analyst, and protect two fixed dashboard-build days per week in the calendar through the pilot.

risk_3: Risk: The COO, burned by the 2023 platform migration cancelled at 60%, withdraws sponsorship at the first slipped milestone before the dashboard proves value. | Type: Standard | Severity: High | Likelihood: Medium | Owner: Operations Analytics Lead | Mitigation: Deliver a visible thin-slice dashboard for 20 vehicles by day 21, hold a 15-minute fortnightly progress check with the COO, and pre-agree that a single missed sprint triggers a replan, not a cancellation.

risk_4: Risk: Consolidating driver and customer data into one dashboard creates a data-access and privacy exposure if utilisation views surface individual driver behaviour to the wrong roles. | Type: Standard | Severity: Medium | Likelihood: Medium | Owner: Fleet Systems Administrator | Mitigation: Apply role-based access so depot managers see depot-level aggregates only, run the data flow past the DPO before go-live, and exclude individual driver names from the COO-facing view.

risk_5: Risk: The dashboard's automated weekly action recommendations (e.g. re-route or re-balance a depot) drift as input data shifts seasonally, so the model keeps surfacing stale or wrong actions and the COO acts on degraded advice without noticing. | Type: AI | Severity: High | Likelihood: Medium | Owner: Operations Analytics Lead | Mitigation: Show the confidence and the underlying figures behind each recommended action, keep a human-in-the-loop sign-off before any action is taken, and run a monthly accuracy check comparing recommended vs realised utilisation, retraining or pausing the recommender if hit-rate drops below 70%.

ai_risk_focus: The dashboard's automated action recommendations can silently drift as fleet and demand patterns shift, leading the COO to act on stale or wrong advice that looks as authoritative as the day it was validated.

ai_risk_specificity: A static report is wrong only if the underlying data is wrong, and that error is visible and stable. An automated recommender can be perfectly accurate at validation and then degrade on its own as conditions change, with no code change and no obvious symptom: the risk is the silent drift between trustworthy-looking output and reality, which only exists once a model is making the call.
